# Analysis of Nintendo's Market Dominance through Global Sales Data

## 1. Project Background & Problem Definition
In the highly competitive video game industry, Nintendo has maintained a unique market position for decades. As a Business student, I developed this data product to explore how Nintendo’s core software titles drive its commercial success. 

**The analytical problem:** Which specific game genres and franchises contribute most to Nintendo's global revenue, and how does market performance vary across different geographic regions (North America, Japan, and Europe)?

## 2. Target User
- **Marketing Strategists**: To understand which game categories resonate best with different regional audiences.
- **Financial Analysts**: To evaluate the stability of Nintendo's revenue streams from its top-performing intellectual properties (IPs).

## 3. Data Source Information
- **Dataset Name**: Video Game Sales 2024
- **Source**: Kaggle (collected from VGChartz)
- **URL**: [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/asaniczka/video-game-sales-2024](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/asaniczka/video-game-sales-2024)
- **Access Date**: April 26, 2026
- **Note**: This dataset was selected because it provides granular regional sales data, which is essential for a business-focused analysis.

## 4. Analytical Workflow (Python)
This project follows a professional data science workflow:
1.**Data Acquisition**: Importing raw data from CSV format.
2.**Data Cleaning**: Filtering for 'Nintendo' as the publisher and removing incomplete records to ensure data integrity.
3.**Data Transformation**: Sorting and aggregating sales figures to identify high-value products.
4.**Visualisation**: Creating intuitive bar charts to communicate insights beyond raw numbers.

## 5. Key Findings & Business Insights
- **IP Power**: A small number of "Evergreen" franchises (like Mario and Pokemon) account for a disproportionate share of total sales, confirming the value of brand loyalty in the gaming industry.
- **Regional Variation**: Preliminary analysis suggests significant differences in genre preference between the Japanese and North American markets.
